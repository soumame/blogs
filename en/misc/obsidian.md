Obsidian is incredibly useful.

## What is Obsidian?
- Its basic functions are the same as Notion's, and you write using [[markdown|Markdown]].
- Notion stores data in a hierarchy, while Obsidian structures data via links between notes.
- To be honest, Notion has more features, but Obsidian runs locally & is noticeably lighter than Notion.
	- It fit me perfectly: I couldn't fully leverage Notion's features and wanted specific functions and APIs.
- You can control appearance and behavior with community-developed themes and plugins.
