In Japan, there is a Minecraft contest called the Minecraft Cup, and similar events exist overseas. In Malaysia, there is an event called MEC (Minecraft Education Challenge). I participated in [MEC 2023](https://mcedumy.com/mec-2023) and won the runner-up prize.

## Malaysia's Minecraft Cup!?

Works that passed the preliminary screening were selected for the final tournament held at the venue sponsored by Sunway Malls.

## Two Years Later in 2025...

So, two years later, I am currently helping to run the Minecraft Cup in Japan. It's recognized that information education and digital making are essential in overseas, especially in Asia. I believe that Minecraft can be extremely useful in these areas.

From my experience, I felt that the level of works was not yet as high as in Japan, and most participants came from urban areas, highlighting regional disparities. (However, Malaysia is a very large country, so bridging this gap is quite challenging.)

Currently, the Minecraft Cup is limited to Japan, and there aren't many applicants from overseas, but I think exploring opportunities abroad could be a good idea in the future.
