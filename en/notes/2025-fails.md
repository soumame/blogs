I do this every year, and I'll do it again this year. It's a session where I expose my personal failures.
By the way, [[2024-fails|last year]] I made a pretty bad mistake: I focused too much on [[blogs/en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]] and didn't think about where I would continue my studies. (I did decide my path safely, but I panicked quite a bit.)

As with last time, I'll briefly look back in this article at the **failures** I had this year (events where the method was poor or circumstances were bad and the goal couldn't be achieved). This year had many successes, but constant glittery self-promotion like “I won such-and-such!” gets boring. There is a lot to learn, yet the internet is full of stories of people who succeeded. ~~Some say that taking pleasure in others' misfortune reduces one's inferiority complex and gives reassurance that ‘they're not really that great, they're just like me’,~~ so let's take a look.
