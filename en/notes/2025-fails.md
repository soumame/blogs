I do this every year, and I'm doing it again this year. This is a session where I expose my personal failures.
By the way, [[2024-fails|last year]] I made a pretty bad mistake: I focused too much on [[blogs/en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]] and didn't think about where I would continue my education. (I ended up deciding my path without issue, but I panicked quite a bit.)

As with last time, I'll briefly look back in this article at the **failures** that happened to me this year (events where the method was poor or the situation bad, and the goal couldn't be achieved). This year had many things that went well, but constant glittery self-promotion like “I won such-and-such award!” gets boring after a while. There is so much to learn, yet the internet is full of stories of people who succeeded. ~~It seems that by taking pleasure in others' misfortunes one can reduce feelings of inferiority and gain comfort by recognizing, 'that person isn't that great after all — they're the same kind of human as me,' so,~~ let's take a look.
