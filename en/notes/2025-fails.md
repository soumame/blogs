I do this every year, and I'm doing it again this year. It's a session where I expose my personal failures.
By the way, [[2024-fails|last year]] I made a pretty bad mistake: I focused too much on [[blogs/en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]] and didn't think about where I'd continue my education. (My path was decided safely, but I panicked quite a bit.)

As with last time, I'll briefly look back on the **failures** that occurred this year (events where the method was poor or the circumstances were bad, preventing me from reaching the goal). This was a year in which many things went well, but I'm getting tired of my own flashy, boast-like posts such as "I won XX!" that I'd be repeating endlessly. There's a lot to learn, yet the internet is full of success stories. ~~Apparently, by taking pleasure in others' misfortunes, one's inferiority complex is reduced and you get reassurance by recognizing "they're not that great after all, they're human like me,"~~ so let's take a look.
