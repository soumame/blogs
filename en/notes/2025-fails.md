I do this every year, and I'm doing it again this year. It's a session where I lay bare my personal failures.
By the way, [[2024-fails|last year]] I made a pretty bad mistake: I focused so much on [[blogs/en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]] that I didn't think about my choice of school. (I did eventually decide on my path safely, but I was pretty panicked.)

As with last time, I plan to briefly look back in an article at the **failures** that happened to me this year (events where the approach was poor or the circumstances were unfavorable, and the objective couldn't be achieved). This year had a lot of successes, but posts like 'I won XX!'—my glossy promotional nonsense—get boring if repeated over and over. There's a lot to learn, yet the internet is full of stories about people who succeeded. ~~They say that by taking pleasure in others' misfortune you can ease your own inferiority complex and realize 'they're not that great after all, they're human like me,' which provides a sense of relief,~~ so let's take a look.
