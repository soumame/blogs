I do this every year, and I'll do it again this year. It's a session where I expose my personal failures.
By the way, [[2024-fails|last year]] I made a pretty bad mistake of focusing too much on [[blogs/en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]] and not thinking about where I'd go next (I did eventually decide my path safely, but I panicked quite a bit).

As with last time, I'll briefly look back on the **failures** I experienced this year (events where the method was poor or the circumstances were unfavorable, so the objective wasn't achieved). This year had many things that went well, but constant glossy self-promotion like “I won XYZ!” gets tiring after a while. There's so much to learn, yet the internet is full of stories of people who succeeded. ~~It seems that taking pleasure in others' misfortune reduces one's inferiority complex and provides reassurance by recognizing "they're not that great; they're just like me," so~~ let's take a look.
