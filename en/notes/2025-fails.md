I do this every year, and I'm doing it again this year. It's a session where I lay bare my personal failures.
By the way, [[2024-fails|去年]] I made a pretty bad mistake: I focused too much on [[blogs/en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|未踏ジュニア]] and didn't think about where I'd continue my studies. (I eventually decided on a path, but I panicked quite a bit.)

As with last time, I'll briefly look back in article form at events this year in which I **failed** (whether because the method was poor or the situation was unfavorable, and the goal wasn't achieved). This year had many things that went well, but constant, glittery self-promotion like "I won X award!" would get boring. There's a lot to learn, yet the internet is full of stories of people who succeeded. ~~It seems that taking pleasure in others' misfortunes reduces one's own inferiority complex and provides reassurance that 'they're not that great after all; they're just human like me,'~~ so let's take a look.
