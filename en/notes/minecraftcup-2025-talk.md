## I Took Part in a Talk Session at the Minecraft Cup

Recently I spoke in a talk session at the [7th Minecraft Cup](https://minecraftcup.com/), held at the University of Tokyo (Interfaculty Initiative in Information Studies, Fukutake Hall), and discussed what I gained through Minecraft under the theme “Learning and the Power of Creation from Minecraft.”

### What is the Minecraft Cup

> [[en/misc/minecraftcup|Minecraft Cup]] is a digital making contest held in Japan for children from elementary to high school age, where participants create theme-based works using Minecraft Education and compete with submissions from across Japan and overseas.

## My Relationship with the Minecraft Cup

[![Image from Gyazo](https://i.gyazo.com/5058cee0ce7389a8b62895f86addd105.jpg)](https://gyazo.com/5058cee0ce7389a8b62895f86addd105)
I won an award at the Minecraft Cup in [[en/accomplishments/minecraftcup-2021|2021]]. There were also times that didn’t end in awards ([[en/accomplishments/minecraftcup-2023|2023]]) and times when we created a project but couldn’t submit it (2019). Sometimes things go well, and sometimes they don’t. The city-building category of the Minecraft Cup is basically team-based, and our team only really came together successfully in 2021 due to internal disagreements, coordination issues, or waning motivation in other years.

https://minecraftcup.com

### What Kind of Team Was It?

The team consisted of people strong in technical skills, people good at design and planning, and people with a broad but shallow range of knowledge beyond Minecraft — basically people who love the "digital making" that the organizing body’s name implies.

We were an uneven group: some didn’t attend school, some couldn’t do what others might consider "normal," and others had exceptional talents. I was originally the kind of person who tended to skip school.

Was that bad? Not really. In 2021, because of COVID-19, we had online classes or no school at all, which gave us free time. That turned out to be a tailwind: the whole team was thrilled to enjoy digital making with Minecraft.

Another characteristic was that we didn’t initially have an instructor. The team formed out of friends playing a game together, not from attending programming classes or being forced by someone. We liked playing Minecraft with friends, discovered a place like this contest, jumped in, and met many kind adults who helped us grow. My parents didn’t force me to play Minecraft, but they didn’t completely forbid it either. They worried about what was best—sometimes saying things like "30 minutes of games a day"—but they were trying to figure out the right approach. In the end, it became an environment that satisfied my intellectual curiosity, and I consider myself lucky for that.


## The Impact of the Minecraft Cup

After that, unbelievably I’m now a university student. I’m at [[en/works/keio|Keio University, Faculty of Environment and Information Studies (SFC)]], a place where the student who used to struggle with school manages to attend almost perfectly. It’s fun.

I was relatively bad at general schoolwork (the typical subjects through high school), but I was confident I could do endless amounts of things I liked. These kinds of individual strengths are increasingly recognized and can be used in university admissions (Keio calls it AO admission). University is truly wonderful—you can learn and dive deep into what you love and are interested in.

### After the Minecraft Cup

Also, the main course of events after the Minecraft Cup was:
- [[en/works/yago.inc|A part-time job at a programming school]]
- After that, based on issues I learned through programming education, [[en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|I applied and was accepted as a Mitou Junior]] and the project I made there won at [[en/accomplishments/teens-apps-awards|App Koshien 2024]]
- And then I enrolled in [[en/works/keio|Keio University, Faculty of Environment and Information Studies (SFC)]]

I think it’s a great result that what I gained from the Minecraft Cup led to these outcomes. Of course, the Minecraft Cup isn’t everything—I’ve met many people outside of it—but it’s certainly one important element.
