## I gave a talk session at the Minecraft Cup
I recently spoke on a talk session at the [7th Minecraft Cup](https://minecraftcup.com/) held at the University of Tokyo (Informatics, Fukutake Hall), discussing "Learning and the Power of Creativity from Minecraft" and what I gained through Minecraft.
### What is the Minecraft Cup
> [[en/misc/minecraftcup|Minecraft Cup]] is a digital making contest held in Japan for children from elementary to high school, where participants use Minecraft Education to create works based on themes and compete by submitting entries from across Japan and abroad.

Along with announcing that I took part in the talk session, I wanted to leave a record here of my relationship with the Minecraft Cup and what I gained from Minecraft (especially from the Minecraft Cup).

At the time of this session, a rough way to describe my connection to the Minecraft Cup is: a software engineer who entered Keio University SFC after receiving awards at the Minecraft Cup. Even calling myself a software engineer feels a bit outdated these days, since AI now writes code, and such titles may fade.

## My involvement with the Minecraft Cup

[![Image from Gyazo](https://i.gyazo.com/5058cee0ce7389a8b62895f86addd105.jpg)](https://gyazo.com/5058cee0ce7389a8b62895f86addd105)
I won an award at the Minecraft Cup in [[en/accomplishments/minecraftcup-2021|2021]]. There were also times when I didn’t ultimately win (like [[en/accomplishments/minecraftcup-2023|2023]]) and times when we made a project but couldn’t submit it (2019). Sometimes things go well, sometimes they don’t. The Minecraft Cup (the town development category, in particular) is basically a team competition, and in my case the only year my team really succeeded was 2021 — we had disagreements, issues coordinating, or lost motivation other years.

https://minecraftcup.com

### What kind of team was it?
We were a group of people with different strengths: those good at technical aspects, those strong in design and planning, and people with broad and shallow knowledge beyond Minecraft. We were a team of people who liked "digital making," as the organizer’s name suggests.

Also, the team members were a bit of a mixed bunch — some weren’t attending school, some couldn’t do what others thought was "normal," and others had exceptional talents. I personally tended to skip school.

Was that a bad thing? Not really. In 2021, with online classes due to COVID and less school structure, we had a lot of free time, which became a tailwind that helped the whole team enjoy digital making in Minecraft.

Another notable point was that we didn’t have a formal instructor at first. The team formed gradually from friends playing games together. We hadn’t attended programming schools or been forced into this; it started from just playing Minecraft with friends, then discovering a place like this, joining it, meeting kind adults, and growing — my parents didn’t force me into Minecraft, but they didn’t completely reject it either. They often said things like "games for 30 minutes a day" (like Kagawa Prefecture’s ordinance), but were thinking about what was best. As a result, I ended up in an environment that satisfied my intellectual curiosity. I consider myself lucky for that.


## Effects of the Minecraft Cup
So what happened after that? Astonishingly, I’m now a university student. I’m studying at [[en/works/keio|Keio University Faculty of Environment and Information Studies (SFC)]], a place where someone who used to struggle with school can now attend almost perfectly regularly. It’s fun.

I was not very good at conventional studies (the usual classes up to high school), but I had confidence that I could endlessly pursue things I liked. Recently, such individuality has been more accepted and can be utilized in university admissions (called AO entrance exams at Keio). University is a wonderful place where you can study and dive deeply into only the things you love and are interested in.

### What happened after the Minecraft Cup
Since the Minecraft Cup, my main path has been:
- Starting with part-time work at a [[en/works/yago.inc|programming school]]
- Then applying to and being accepted as a [[en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]] project based on issues I learned about through programming education, and winning an award at the [[en/accomplishments/teens-apps-awards|Teens Apps Awards 2024]] with something I made there
- And then entering [[en/works/keio|Keio University SFC]]

I think it’s great that what I gained from the Minecraft Cup has turned into these outcomes. Of course, the Minecraft Cup isn’t everything, and I’ve met many people elsewhere too, but it’s definitely one important element.
