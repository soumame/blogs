## I Spoke on a Talk Session at the Minecraft Cup
I recently appeared on a talk session at the [7th Minecraft Cup](https://minecraftcup.com/) held at the University of Tokyo (Interfaculty Initiative in Information Studies, Fukutake Hall), discussing "Learning and the Power of Creation from Minecraft" and what I gained through Minecraft.
### What is the Minecraft Cup
> [[en/misc/minecraftcup|Minecraft Cup]] is a digital making contest held in Japan for children from elementary school to high school, where participants use Minecraft Education to create works based on themes and compete by submitting entries from across the country and overseas.

Along with announcing that I appeared on the talk session, I wanted to leave a record here about my relationship with the Minecraft Cup and what I gained from Minecraft (especially from the Minecraft Cup).

## My Involvement with the Minecraft Cup

[![Image from Gyazo](https://i.gyazo.com/5058cee0ce7389a8b62895f86addd105.jpg)](https://gyazo.com/5058cee0ce7389a8b62895f86addd105)
I won an award at the Minecraft Cup in [[en/accomplishments/minecraftcup-2021|2021]]. I also had editions that didn’t ultimately receive awards ([[en/accomplishments/minecraftcup-2023|2023]]) and an edition where we made a project but couldn’t submit it (2019). There are times things go well and times they don’t. The city-building category of the Minecraft Cup is basically a team competition, and my team only succeeded in 2021 — other times we had disagreements, poor coordination, or lost motivation.

https://minecraftcup.com

### What kind of team was it?
We gathered people who were good at the technical side, people strong in design and planning, and people with broad but shallow knowledge beyond Minecraft — basically, a team of people who liked "digital making," which is even in the name of the organizing body.

Also, many team members were a bit unconventional: some didn’t attend school regularly, some couldn’t do what others call “normal,” and some had remarkable talents. I myself was originally somewhat prone to not attending school.

Was that a bad thing? Not necessarily. The 2021 Minecraft Cup took place during COVID, with online classes and, for some, no school at all, so people had more free time. That tailwind allowed the whole team to happily enjoy digital making with Minecraft.

Another notable point is that we didn’t have an instructor at first. The team formed organically from friends playing a game together; none of us attended programming schools or were forced into it. We just liked playing Minecraft with friends, found a place like this, jumped in, and encountered many kind adults who helped us grow. My parents didn’t explicitly set out to make me play Minecraft, but they also didn’t completely reject it. They often talked about things like limiting game time, but they were figuring out what would be best, and in the end the environment satisfied my intellectual curiosity. I consider myself lucky for that.


## Effects of the Minecraft Cup
So what happened afterward? Remarkably, I’m now a university student. I’m studying at [[en/works/keio|Keio University, Faculty of Environment and Information Studies (SFC)]], a place where someone who used to struggle with school is now able to attend almost perfectly — it’s fun.

I was generally not good at typical school-style study (up through high school classes), but I was confident I could endlessly pursue things I liked. Recently, such individuality is being recognized, and it can be leveraged in university admissions (Keio calls it AO admission). University is truly a wonderful place where you can study only what you love and pursue it deeply.

### Progress after the Minecraft Cup
Since the Minecraft Cup, my main path has been:
- Starting with a [[en/works/yago.inc|part-time job at a programming school]]
- Then applying to and being accepted as a [[en/accomplishments/mitoujr-2024|Mitou Junior]], based on issues I learned about through programming education, and winning an award at [[en/accomplishments/teens-apps-awards|App Koshien 2024]] for a project I built there
- After that, enrolling in [[en/works/keio|Keio University, Faculty of Environment and Information Studies (SFC)]].

I think it’s great that what I gained from the Minecraft Cup led to these outcomes. Of course, the Minecraft Cup isn’t everything — I met many people and had other influences — but it definitely played a role.
