### Thread Proposal — Can You Write a Blog Through Dialogue?

In Discord's #openclaw, Somame posed the question, “Couldn't the chat itself be turned into a blog?” The conversation began by exploring the possibility of turning existing social media threads into articles while inviting collaborators, in an era where AI is changing how we write.

Somame
> I’ll try creating a blog using a thread.
>
> I’ve been writing a blog recently, but I wondered whether AI might change how blogs are written in the first place. So I’m going to try creating a blog using the currently popular chat interface.
>
> I’ll try creating a blog through dialogue with OpenClaw.
>
> Since it’s made through dialogue, I don’t know how the quality of the blog will turn out, but I feel like I’ll be able to write much more quickly.
>
> Also, rather than starting from scratch, doing it on an existing SNS could let you invite various people and write a blog together — wouldn’t that be interesting? So I’ll give it a try.
>
> By the way, the scribe for this blog is the popular OpenClaw I’m hosting at home.

### OpenClaw Scribe and Initial Briefing

OpenClaw joined as the scribe, and they aligned on the theme, flow, and how to collect materials. Somame checked whether there were precedents and grew more convinced this was a worthwhile experiment to do now.

OpenClaw Scribe
> Hello, I’m the OpenClaw scribe! Turning a dialogue into a blog sounds super interesting. What themes and flow are you imagining? Or shall we start by chatting casually to gather material?

Somame
> Are there many examples of having OpenClaw write blogs?

OpenClaw Scribe
> Not a ton of cases. Among published examples, I’ve seen experiments where OpenClaw was run on Discord or Telegram and the conversation logs were turned into blogs, but it doesn’t seem to have become a "standard workflow" yet. On the other hand, that means doing it now could make you stand out. The raw logs have their own flavor, and lightly polishing them into a Dialogue Blog style is also an option.

### Workflow Confirmation and First Draft Drop

Somame instructed the use of the "/discord_dialogue_blog" slash command, and OpenClaw confirmed the required inputs. They proceeded to compile the first draft into `blog.md` and shared it to the channel in one go.

OpenClaw Scribe
> Got it. Theme: "Turning OpenClaw dialogues into a blog." Format: organize the Discord conversation log by topic and follow the frontmatter template. Should the entire thread be the source for the log? Do you want to include other channels or past interactions? Also let me know if it’s okay to use the date of this conversation as the publication date.

Somame
> /discord_dialogue_blog

OpenClaw Scribe
> I’ve compiled the blog draft into `blog.md`. The frontmatter follows the template, and the body is structured into four sections: Introduction → Joining → Arrangement → Next Steps. I can capture additional conversations and append them if needed. If the review is OK, I’ll finalize it for publication, but tell me if there are other logs or annotations you want to include.

Somame
> Can you attach the file to Discord?

*The OpenClaw scribe attached `blog.md` and shared the first draft.*

Somame
> Create the blog using discord-dialogue-blog and send it.

OpenClaw Scribe
> The `blog.md` I just made is exactly that discord-dialogue-blog version. Do you want me to paste it back into the thread again, or adjust the content and re-output it?

### Rule Update and Turning into a Skill

They reviewed the operating rules further, and Somame defined the procedure as a new Skill. OpenClaw reviewed the changes and prepared for the next regeneration.

Somame
> I changed the rules, so regenerate and send it.

OpenClaw Scribe
> Understood. Can you tell me the new rule details? I’ll regenerate the blog accordingly and repost it.

Somame
> I made it as a proper skill.

OpenClaw Scribe
> Got it. I’ll reorganize the conversation log following the updated skill procedure.
